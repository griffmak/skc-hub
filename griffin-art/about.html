The Artist's Story

Meanwhile, in a paint-stained studio somewhere...

I've been painting since before I could write my name. What started as crayon murals on the living room wall became a lifelong obsession with color, form, and storytelling.

Every painting is a conversation. Sometimes it's a shout, sometimes it's a whisper — but it always starts with a feeling I can't put into words, so I put it on canvas instead.

The Process

I work mostly in acrylics and mixed media. There's something about the speed of acrylics that matches how I think — fast, layered, building up color and texture until the painting tells me it's done.

I don't plan too far ahead. Most pieces start with a color or a feeling, and I chase it until something emerges. Sometimes that takes an afternoon, sometimes it takes months.

Influences

Comics, street art, music, the city at night. I grew up on Marvel comics and graffiti-covered subway cars. That energy — bold, unapologetic, alive — is what I try to bring to every piece.

If a painting doesn't make you feel something, I haven't done my job.
